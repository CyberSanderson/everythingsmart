Top Accessories Every 3D Printer Owner Needs

If you're passionate about 3D printing, you know that having the right accessories can make a world of difference. The best 3D printer alone isn't enough—adding quality tools and upgrades will help you get better prints, reduce downtime, and enjoy a smoother workflow. Whether you’re just starting or already a seasoned maker, this guide covers the must-have accessories to take your 3D printing game to the next level.

1. Quality Build Surface or Bed Adhesive

A reliable build surface is crucial for print adhesion and removing finished parts without damage. Without it, your prints can warp, detach mid-print, or leave residue on the bed.

Types of Build Surfaces

  • PEI Sheets: Durable and reusable, PEI (Polyetherimide) sheets provide excellent adhesion for most filaments and are easy to clean.
  • Magnetic Flex Plates: These attach magnetically and allow you to flex the plate to pop off prints easily, reducing damage.
  • Glass Beds: Often paired with adhesives, glass provides a flat surface and can withstand higher temps.
  • Glue Stick & Hairspray: Budget-friendly adhesives that improve stickiness on glass or other beds.

How to Choose the Right Build Surface

Consider your filament types and printing habits. PEI sheets are great all-rounders. Magnetic flex plates add convenience for easy print removal. For ABS or other filaments prone to warping, enclosed beds with glass and adhesive sprays are common.

Maintenance Tips

Clean build surfaces regularly with isopropyl alcohol to maintain adhesion. Avoid scratching the surface, and replace the adhesive layer if prints start to peel off.

2. Precision Calipers

Accurate measurements are vital in 3D printing, especially for calibration and quality control. Digital calipers help you measure filament diameter, printed parts, and clearances with high precision.

Why They Matter

Filament diameter affects extrusion rates; measuring it helps tune your printer settings to reduce over- or under-extrusion. Checking printed parts ensures tolerances are met, which is critical for mechanical assemblies or fitment.

Choosing Calipers

Look for digital calipers with at least 0.01mm accuracy. Features like an easy-to-read LCD, zero setting, and metric/inch conversions make them more user-friendly.

✅ Recommended: Digital Calipers on Amazon

3. Nozzle Cleaning Kit

Nozzle clogs can stall or ruin prints. A cleaning kit with needles or wires tailored to nozzle sizes helps maintain consistent extrusion.

Types of Cleaning Tools

  • Nozzle Cleaning Needles: Thin, stiff wires sized to nozzle diameters to clear blockages.
  • Brushes and Picks: For removing filament residue around the nozzle and hotend.
  • Cold Pull Tools: A method using nylon filament to pull out debris inside the nozzle.

Maintenance Tips

Regularly inspect your nozzle and clean when you notice extrusion issues. Avoid using sharp objects that might damage the nozzle or heater block.

✅ Recommended: Nozzle Cleaning Kit on Amazon

4. Spare Nozzles

Having spare nozzles on hand minimizes downtime caused by wear, damage, or experimenting with different nozzle sizes.

Why Keep Spares?

Nozzles wear down over time, especially when printing abrasive filaments like carbon fiber or glow-in-the-dark materials. Switching diameters can optimize for speed or detail depending on your project.

Types of Nozzles

  • Brass Nozzles: Most common and cost-effective.
  • Hardened Steel Nozzles: Durable for abrasive filaments.
  • Ruby-Tipped Nozzles: Premium, very wear-resistant but expensive.

✅ Recommended: Spare Nozzles on Amazon

5. Filament Storage Solutions

Filament quality depends heavily on moisture content. Exposure to humidity causes filament to absorb water, leading to poor print quality, bubbling, or brittle parts.

Storage Options

  • Dry Boxes: Sealed containers with desiccants that maintain low humidity.
  • Airtight Containers: Store spools with silica gel packs for moisture absorption.
  • Filament Dryers: Devices that actively dry filament before and during printing.

Best Practices

Store filament spools immediately after use. Use filament dryers for sensitive materials like Nylon or TPU. Monitor humidity levels in your printing area for consistent results.

✅ Recommended: Filament Dryer on Amazon

6. Bed Leveling Tools

A level bed is critical for good first layers, adhesion, and overall print quality. Poor leveling leads to warping or failed prints.

Manual vs. Automatic Leveling

  • Feeler Gauges: Traditional tool to manually set the nozzle-to-bed gap precisely.
  • BLTouch & Sensors: Automatic leveling probes that measure multiple points and compensate during printing.

Which to Choose?

Manual leveling is cheap and effective but requires practice. Automatic sensors save time and increase consistency but add cost and installation effort.

7. Cleaning and Finishing Tools

Post-processing is essential for polished prints. Basic finishing tools improve aesthetics and remove print artifacts.

Common Finishing Tools

  • Deburring Tools: Remove rough edges and support marks.
  • Sanding Sticks & Papers: Smooth surfaces with varying grits.
  • Flush Cutters: Trim supports cleanly.

Investing in these tools saves time and delivers professional-looking prints.

✅ Recommended: 3D Print Finishing Tools on Amazon

8. OctoPrint or Remote Monitoring Solutions

Monitoring your prints remotely adds convenience and safety. OctoPrint is a popular open-source platform to control your printer from a web browser or phone.

Benefits of Remote Monitoring

  • Start, stop, or pause prints without being next to the machine
  • Receive alerts on print progress or issues
  • Stream live video to check print quality

Getting Started

OctoPrint runs on a Raspberry Pi connected to your printer. Many commercial printers offer built-in Wi-Fi monitoring as well.

✅ Recommended: Raspberry Pi for OctoPrint

9. How to Maintain Your 3D Printer Accessories

Regular maintenance keeps your accessories working efficiently and extends their lifespan.

  • Clean build surfaces with isopropyl alcohol after every few prints.
  • Store nozzles in dry, dust-free containers.
  • Check and calibrate calipers periodically.
  • Replace worn or damaged accessories promptly.

Consistent upkeep saves time and money in the long run.

10. FAQs About 3D Printer Accessories

Q: How often should I replace my 3D printer nozzle?

A: Typically, every 3-6 months depending on usage and filament type. Abrasive filaments wear nozzles faster.

Q: Can I use hairspray instead of a PEI sheet for bed adhesion?

A: Yes, hairspray can improve adhesion on glass beds, but PEI sheets offer a more durable and cleaner solution.

Q: What’s the best way to store filament spools?

A: Use airtight containers or dry boxes with desiccant packs to keep filament dry and prevent moisture damage.

Q: Is OctoPrint difficult to set up?

A: It requires some technical setup with a Raspberry Pi, but plenty of tutorials and community support are available.

Final Thoughts

Investing in the right 3D printer accessories can transform your printing experience—improving quality, saving time, and reducing frustration. Start with the essentials like a quality build surface and precision calipers, then add tools and upgrades based on your workflow and goals.

Remember, every maker’s needs are different. Use this guide to prioritize accessories that best suit your projects and budget, and watch your prints come to life like never before!
